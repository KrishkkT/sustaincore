Air Quality FAQs

Clarifying the complexities of atmospheric monitoring.

Typically, stack monitoring is required once every quarter or once every six months, as specified in your Consent to Operate (CTO). For highly polluting industries (Red Category), continuous emission monitoring systems (CEMS) are mandatory for real-time reporting to the CPCB/SPCB servers.
Isokinetic sampling ensures that the sample of air taken from the stack represents the exact velocity and particulate concentration of the gas in the stack. If the sampling speed is too fast or too slow, the particulate data will be skewed. We use digital V-probe systems to ensure perfect isokinetic ratios.
Per the National Ambient Air Quality Standards (2009), the 24-hour limit for PM2.5 is 60 µg/m³ and the annual limit is 40 µg/m³. For PM10, the 24-hour limit is 100 µg/m³. Non-compliance often triggers additional dust mitigation requirements from the local pollution control board.
