Why modern reporting is difficult?

We address the core challenges of corporate disclosure.

Regulatory Complexity

BRSR, GRI, SASB, and TCFD all have different requirements. Keeping track of overlapping metrics is a significant administrative burden.

Data Fragmentation

Sustainability data often sits in siloed departments—HR, Operations, Finance, and Supply Chain—making it hard to create a unified narrative.

Audit Readiness

Investors now demand "assurance" on ESG data. Reports that lack a clear data-trail are increasingly rejected by institutional capital.

Frequently Asked Questions

Clarifying the complexities of ESG disclosure.

Currently, SEBI mandates BRSR for the top 1000 listed entities by market capitalization in India. However, many unlisted companies are adopting it voluntarily to satisfy supply chain requirements from global partners or to prepare for future IPOs and private equity due diligence.
Double materiality considers two dimensions: how sustainability issues affect your company's financial value (Financial Materiality) and how your company's activities impact people and the environment (Impact Materiality). Our protocol covers both to meet EU CSRD and GRI requirements.
A full, framework-aligned report typically takes 3 to 5 months. This includes stakeholder engagement, data collection across siloed departments, verification, narrative drafting, and design. Early preparation is key to ensuring audit-ready data.
SEBI now requires "Reasonable Assurance" for the BRSR Core attributes for the top 150 listed entities (from FY 2023-24), scaling to the top 1000 over the next few years. We design your data pipeline to be inherently auditable by any global tier-1 assurance provider.
