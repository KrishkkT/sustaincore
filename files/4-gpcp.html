Regulatory Framework

Industry categories under GPCB

GPCB classifies every industry by its Pollution Index (PI) score — a composite of air, water and hazardous waste impact. Your category determines consent validity, renewal frequency and compliance obligations.

Red
PI ≥ 60
Highly Polluting
Chemicals, pharmaceuticals, dyes, tanneries, smelters. Strictest compliance. CEMS mandatory. CTO valid 5 years. Frequent GPCB inspections.
Orange
PI 41–59
Moderately Polluting
Engineering, food processing, textiles (non-dyeing), packaging. Moderate ETP/STP requirements. CTO valid 5 years. Annual environmental statement required.
Green
PI 21–40
Less Polluting
Light manufacturing, assembling, cold storage, bakeries. Simplified consent process. CTO valid 10 years. Lower compliance burden but registration required.
White
PI ≤ 20
Non-Polluting
IT, education, trading, services. One-time registration. Perpetual validity CTO. No routine GPCB inspection. Minimal compliance documentation.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Operating after CTO expiry is an offence under the Water Act 1974 and Air Act 1981 and can attract a show-cause notice, closure direction and prosecution. GPCB also has the authority to seal plant and machinery. Filing before expiry — ideally 30–90 days before — is essential to avoid any gap. We track renewal windows for all clients and initiate the process 90 days before expiry.

Yes. Hazardous Waste Rules 2016 do not prescribe a minimum quantity threshold for authorization. Any unit generating waste listed in Schedule I, II or III of the Rules requires authorization. Common examples include spent solvents, ETP sludge, used oils, electroplating waste and paint sludge. We first conduct a waste stream audit to identify all applicable schedules before preparing the authorization application.

Processing times vary by category and GPCB regional office workload. A well-prepared application for Green/Orange category typically receives consent within 30–60 days of submission. Red category applications involve a site inspection and additional scrutiny, typically taking 60–90 days. Incomplete applications or query responses from GPCB can extend timelines significantly — which is why we ensure first-time accuracy in document preparation.

Any expansion, addition of new products, installation of additional DG sets or boilers, or significant change in raw material or process requires a consent amendment. Operating an expanded facility without amendment is a violation. We assess whether your proposed change requires a fresh CTE or an amendment to existing consent, and manage the amendment application accordingly.

e-Gujcop is GPCB's online portal for all consent and authorization applications, compliance report submissions and fee payments. All GPCB applications must now be submitted online through e-Gujcop — physical submissions are no longer accepted. We manage the complete portal workflow including account setup, application filling, document upload, fee calculation and real-time status tracking on your behalf.
