Why it matters

CTE is not optional.
It is the first legal gate.

No industry in Gujarat can begin construction, lay foundation or install any equipment without a valid Consent to Establish from GPCB. The consequences of bypassing this requirement are severe.

Legal Prerequisite
Under the Water (Prevention & Control of Pollution) Act 1974 and the Air (Prevention & Control of Pollution) Act 1981, commencing establishment activities without CTE attracts prosecution, site closure orders and financial penalties that can exceed project costs.
Foundation for CTO
CTO â€” the consent required before operations begin â€” cannot be granted without a valid CTE. Delays or deficiencies in CTE cascade into delayed commercial production, making first-time accuracy in the CTE application critical.
Financial & Insurance Risk
Banks, NBFCs and project insurers require proof of GPCB CTE before disbursing project finance. Missing or defective CTE can freeze loan drawdowns mid-construction and trigger lender recall clauses.
