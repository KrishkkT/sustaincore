Types of EC

Environmental Clearancecategories we manage

From first-time greenfield approvals to expansions, amendments and validity extensions â€” we cover the complete EC lifecycle.

New Project
Fresh Environmental Clearance
First-time EC for greenfield or new projects crossing EIA Notification Schedule thresholds. Full EIA process required â€” TOR, baseline, EIA report, public hearing, appraisal and EC grant. Valid 5 years (10 years for mining).
Capacity Expansion
EC for Project Expansion
Required when existing projects exceed 25% capacity expansion or add new products/activities crossing Schedule thresholds. May require updated baseline data and supplementary EIA. Managed through Parivesh as an expansion EC application.
Change in Scope
EC Amendment
For changes within existing EC parameters â€” technology modification, process changes, raw material substitution or minor capacity adjustments not crossing expansion thresholds. Filed through Parivesh amendment module with supporting justification report.
EC Running Out
Validity Extension
EC validity can be extended if the project is not commissioned before the 5-year expiry. Extension application requires evidence of project progress, updated compliance status and justification for delay. Must be filed before EC lapses to avoid fresh EC.
Ownership Change
EC Transfer
When a project changes ownership through sale, merger, demerger or inheritance, the EC must be formally transferred to the new entity. We manage the transfer application with all supporting corporate documentation.
Ongoing Obligation
Post-EC Compliance Reporting
Six-monthly compliance reports (Half-yearly and Annual reports to MoEFCC/SEIAA and regional office) are mandatory throughout the project lifecycle. Non-submission attracts legal action. We prepare and file all reports on schedule.

Environmental Clearance (EC) and GPCB Consent are separate approvals from different authorities. EC is issued by MoEFCC (for Category A projects) or SEIAA Gujarat (Category B), and authorises the project itself under the EIA Notification 2006. GPCB Consent to Establish (CTE) and Consent to Operate (CTO) are issued by Gujarat Pollution Control Board under the Water Act and Air Act, and govern the pollution control standards the facility must meet. Both are required â€” EC before construction, GPCB CTE/CTO before operations. They have different documentation, different timelines and different post-approval obligations. We manage both processes and ensure they proceed on parallel tracks.

Yes, EC validity can be extended. For new projects, the standard validity is 5 years (10 for mining), extendable by up to 5 more years by MoEFCC or SEIAA. The extension application must be filed before the EC lapses â€” a lapsed EC cannot be extended and a fresh EC process is then required. The extension application must demonstrate project progress, ongoing EMP compliance and reasons for the delay. We file extension applications well before expiry with a strong progress justification to maximise approval probability.

Non-submission of six-monthly compliance reports is a violation of EC conditions and can trigger a show-cause notice from MoEFCC or SEIAA, and potentially a referral to the state pollution control board. Repeated non-compliance can result in suspension or revocation of the EC. The reports are also monitored by the Regional Offices of MoEFCC, which conduct periodic site inspections. Missing a report deadline is treated as evidence of non-compliance with EC conditions â€” regardless of whether the actual environmental performance is satisfactory.
