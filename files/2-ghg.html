Why it matters

You cannot reduce
what you have not measured.

A GHG inventory is not just a disclosure requirement — it is the management tool that tells you where your emissions come from and where reduction opportunities exist.

Regulatory Foundation
CBAM, BRSR, CDP, SBTi and all major ESG frameworks require a verified GHG inventory. Without one, you cannot satisfy any of these requirements credibly. The inventory is the starting point for everything.
Reduction Prioritisation
A complete Scope 1, 2 and 3 inventory shows you exactly where emissions concentrate — enabling targeted investment in the highest-impact reduction measures rather than spreading effort thinly.
Supply Chain Compliance
Over 280 global corporations now request verified Scope 3 data from their suppliers. A credible, verified GHG inventory is becoming a procurement prerequisite for supplying these organisations.
What we do

Complete GHG inventory —
from boundary setting to verification

We build GHG inventories that satisfy CDP, BRSR, SBTi, CBAM and ISO 14064 requirements simultaneously — collected once, applied everywhere.

Boundary & Scope Setting
We define organisational boundaries (operational control or equity share approach), determine which emission scopes apply and identify all emission sources across your operations and value chain.
Scope 1 — Direct Emissions
We calculate all direct emissions from fuel combustion (boilers, furnaces, vehicles), industrial processes (chemical reactions, smelting), fugitive emissions (refrigerants, methane leaks) and owned biomass combustion.
Scope 2 — Purchased Energy
We calculate electricity-related emissions using both location-based (grid emission factor) and market-based (contractual instruments, I-REC) methods, as required by CDP and GHG Protocol.
Scope 3 — Value Chain Emissions
We screen all 15 Scope 3 categories, identify the highest-emission categories for your business model, and calculate them using a combination of primary supplier data and peer-reviewed emission factor databases.
Verification Preparation & Audit Trail
All calculations are documented with full methodology, emission factor citations, uncertainty analysis and data quality ratings — producing an audit trail that ISO 14064 verifiers can validate without extensive rework.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Scope 1 covers direct emissions from sources your organisation owns or controls — boilers, company vehicles, industrial process emissions and fugitive releases. Scope 2 covers indirect emissions from purchased energy — electricity, heat, steam and cooling. Scope 3 covers all other indirect emissions across your value chain — upstream supply chain (purchased goods and services, capital goods, fuel and energy activities), direct operations (business travel, employee commuting, leased assets) and downstream (use of sold products, end-of-life treatment, franchises, investments). For most organisations, Scope 3 represents 70-90% of total emissions.

The GHG Protocol requires inventories to be complete, consistent, transparent, accurate and relevant. Accuracy standards depend on data quality: primary data (direct measurement, utility bills, supplier invoices) gives ±5-10% uncertainty; secondary data (spend-based estimates, industry average factors) gives ±30-50% uncertainty. We prioritise primary data for your highest-emission sources and use secondary data only where primary is unavailable — and we document uncertainty explicitly for every category, as ISO 14064 requires.

Scope 3 is not always required for regulatory compliance but is increasingly expected by investors, customers and frameworks. SBTi requires Scope 3 targets if Scope 3 exceeds 40% of total emissions. CDP Climate awards significant scoring credit for Scope 3 disclosure. BRSR requires Scope 3 narrative disclosure. GRI Topic Standards require value chain data. Most importantly, Scope 3 is where your reduction opportunities and customer/investor pressure actually concentrate — understanding it is a business necessity, not just a reporting exercise.

We use the most authoritative, peer-reviewed emission factor databases available: IPCC AR6 for global warming potentials, DEFRA UK Government GHG Conversion Factors for transport and supply chain, CEA/MoP India grid emission factors for electricity, ecoinvent for product lifecycle data, IPCC 2006 guidelines for combustion factors, and sector-specific databases for industry processes. All factors are cited, dated and linked to source publications in our calculation documentation — so any verifier, auditor or investor can independently confirm every number.
