Why it matters

Materiality is the cornerstone
of every credible ESG report.

Regulators, investors and frameworks all require it. Without a rigorous, defensible materiality process, your sustainability disclosures lack credibility — and your strategy lacks direction.

01
Mandatory for BRSR & GRI
SEBI’s BRSR framework and GRI Universal Standards 2021 both require a documented materiality process. Without it, your disclosure is structurally incomplete and vulnerable to regulatory and investor challenge.
02
CSRD / ESRS Double Materiality
The EU Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive requires double materiality — assessing both financial materiality (impact on the company) and impact materiality (company’s impact on people and planet). Indian subsidiaries of EU companies face this obligation now.
03
Investor Credibility Signal
ESG rating agencies including MSCI, Sustainalytics and EcoVadis evaluate the rigour of your materiality process. A board-approved, stakeholder-validated matrix signals mature ESG governance and directly improves your ratings.
04
Strategic Resource Allocation
A well-executed materiality assessment prevents the common error of disclosing everything equally. It tells your leadership exactly where to invest sustainability resources for maximum business and stakeholder impact.

FAQ

Frequently asked
questions

Single materiality (used by BRSR, GRI and ISSB) focuses on topics that are material to the financial performance and strategy of the company itself — essentially, what ESG factors affect your business. Double materiality (required by the EU’s CSRD and ESRS) adds a second dimension: the company’s own impacts on people and the environment, regardless of whether those impacts affect financial performance. CSRD-applicable companies must assess and disclose on both axes. We conduct both types and, where applicable, integrate them into a single assessment process to avoid duplication.

GRI 2021 does not prescribe a fixed refresh cycle, but the standard expectation — reinforced by ESRS — is a full reassessment every 2–3 years, with annual monitoring for significant changes. Triggers requiring an interim update include major M&A activity, entry into new geographies or business lines, significant regulatory changes, or material stakeholder incidents. We provide a monitoring protocol as part of every engagement so you always know when a refresh is due and what to watch for in the interim.

SEBI’s BRSR framework does not use the term “materiality assessment” explicitly, but it requires disclosure on material risks, stakeholder engagement and the principle-wise assessment of the NGRBC 9 principles — all of which are substantively informed by a formal materiality process. Moreover, BRSR Core requires assured disclosures on KPIs that should be grounded in material topic identification. Investors reviewing BRSR quality increasingly expect to see a documented materiality process as evidence of genuine ESG governance rather than box-ticking. A GRI-aligned materiality assessment fully satisfies this expectation and strengthens your BRSR considerably.

A single well-designed materiality process, conducted to GRI 2021 standards with ESRS overlay, simultaneously satisfies the requirements of BRSR, GRI, CSRD, ISSB and EcoVadis. The key is building the topic universe to span all four frameworks’ topic lists from the start, and documenting methodology choices that meet each framework’s specific requirements. We deliver a cross-reference map showing how each material topic satisfies each applicable framework’s materiality disclosure requirement.

Our standard engagement covers four stakeholder groups: internal (employees and senior management), commercial (customers and suppliers), financial (investors, lenders and analysts) and societal (local community representatives, NGOs and regulators where accessible). Within each group we target a statistically sufficient sample using structured digital surveys, supplemented by targeted interviews for key informants. For most engagements this means 50–200 survey respondents and 8–15 in-depth interviews. All responses are anonymised, weighted by group and documented for assurance purposes.
