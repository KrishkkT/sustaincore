FAQ

Frequently asked questions

GRI’s with-reference option means your report presents information aligned with the GRI Standards but does not claim full compliance. It is less stringent than in-accordance reporting, which requires all mandatory disclosures to be complete and accurate. Most first-time reporters begin with with-reference and move to in-accordance within two reporting cycles as data quality and systems improve. We recommend the level appropriate to your current data maturity and stakeholder expectations.

GRI 2021 introduced significant changes: the Universal Standards now apply to all organisations (not optional topic-by-topic), stakeholder inclusiveness and materiality determination are more rigorous, double materiality is embedded, and the GRI Sector Standards provide more specific requirements for high-impact sectors (mining, oil & gas, agriculture are already published, with more sectors to follow). The 2021 standards also integrate more explicitly with TCFD and ISSB frameworks, making GRI 2021 the most future-proof reporting choice.

First-time GRI reports take 12–18 weeks from kickoff. This includes stakeholder engagement and materiality determination (4-6 weeks), data collection (4-6 weeks), drafting and review (3-4 weeks) and assurance/publication (2-4 weeks). Companies with existing BRSR data can often move faster because much of the environmental and social performance data is already collected. Subsequent annual cycles run in 8-10 weeks.

Assurance is not mandatory under GRI Standards but is strongly recommended for credibility with institutional investors and ESG rating agencies. Limited assurance (ISAE 3000) is achievable in 3-4 weeks and significantly increases stakeholder confidence. Reasonable assurance provides higher credibility but requires more preparation time. We build all GRI reports to assurance-ready standard regardless of whether formal assurance is engaged — this means you can add assurance at any point without rework.
