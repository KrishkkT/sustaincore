CDP Water disclosure

Understanding CDP Water

CDP Water questionnaire assesses water accounting, risk assessment, governance, strategy, and targets. Responses are scored A to D- with public disclosure of scores.

Water Accounting

Detailed reporting of water withdrawals by source, consumption, discharge by destination, and water recycling. Data quality, verification, and boundary coverage are assessed.

Risk Assessment

Identification of water-related risks — physical (scarcity, flooding, quality), regulatory (permits, pricing, allocation), and reputational. Assessment of financial implications and response strategies.

Business Strategy

Integration of water into business strategy, scenario analysis, water-related opportunities, and value chain engagement. Board oversight and management accountability.

Targets & Goals

Water quantity and quality targets, progress tracking, and alignment with contextual water targets. Science-based approaches and basin-level considerations.

Governance

Board-level oversight, management responsibility, incentives linked to water performance, and integration with enterprise risk management.

CDP scoring

Understanding CDP Water scores

CDP scores companies from A (leadership) to D- (disclosure) based on the depth and quality of their response. Score improvement requires systematic enhancement of water management practices and disclosure quality.

A / A-Leadership
B / B-Management
C / C-Awareness
D / D-Disclosure
A

Leadership (A / A-)

Comprehensive water accounting, detailed risk assessment with financial quantification, ambitious targets with progress, board oversight, value chain engagement, and demonstrated best practices. A-List recognition.

B

Management (B / B-)

Good water data coverage, risk assessment process in place, water targets set, management accountability established, and actions taken to address identified risks.

C

Awareness (C / C-)

Basic water accounting, awareness of water risks, some water policies in place, but limited targets, incomplete risk assessment, or gaps in management approach.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

CDP Water questionnaire typically opens in April and closes in late July each year. Companies have approximately 3-4 months to complete their response. However, data collection and response preparation should begin earlier — ideally January-February for data gathering, March-April for risk assessment and response drafting, May-June for internal review and approval, with submission in July. Scores are released in December.

Companies can self-select to respond to CDP Water even without investor or customer requests. Self-selected responses receive the same scoring and public disclosure as requested responses. Benefits include: proactive demonstration of water stewardship to investors, improved ESG ratings (which pull CDP data), competitive differentiation with customers, and internal discipline for water management improvement. For water-intensive industries, proactive disclosure is increasingly expected.

CDP Water requires: total water withdrawal by source (surface water, groundwater, municipal, rainwater, etc.), water consumption (withdrawal minus discharge), water discharge by destination and quality, water recycled/reused, and breakdown by facility or business segment. Beyond data, you need documentation of water risks, governance practices, policies, targets, and actions. A water audit provides the foundation data; our assessment builds the risk and governance components.

WRI Aqueduct is a global water risk mapping tool that provides basin-level risk indicators for any location. We use Aqueduct to assess: baseline water stress (demand vs. supply), drought risk, flood risk, seasonal variability, groundwater depletion, and regulatory/reputational risk. By mapping your facility coordinates against Aqueduct, we identify which sites face elevated water risk and require priority attention. This analysis directly supports CDP responses.

A-List requires excellence across all scoring categories: complete and verified water data, comprehensive risk assessment with financial quantification, ambitious targets with demonstrated progress, board-level oversight, integration into business strategy, value chain engagement, and evidence of water stewardship leadership. Most companies require 2-3 years of progressive improvement to reach A-List. Our roadmap identifies the specific gaps preventing A-List and prioritises actions for score progression.

BRSR Principle 6 requires water withdrawal, consumption, and discharge reporting similar to CDP. A strong CDP Water response provides the data and narrative needed for BRSR water disclosures. The water risk assessment also supports BRSR's requirement to discuss environmental risks. We design our data collection to serve both CDP and BRSR requirements, avoiding duplication of effort.
