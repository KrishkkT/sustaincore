E-Waste Authorization
& EPR Registration
Under E-Waste Rules 2022

The E-Waste (Management) Rules 2022 — effective April 2023 — have fundamentally reformed India's e-waste compliance landscape. Every manufacturer, producer, importer, recycler, refurbisher and dismantler of electrical and electronic equipment must register on the SPCB EPR Portal and meet annual recycling targets. In Gujarat, GPCB authorization is additionally required for recyclers and dismantlers. We manage end-to-end e-waste compliance for all entity types.

Why it matters

E-waste compliance has been
fundamentally reformed.

The 2022 Rules replaced the 2016 framework with a stricter EPR-based regime. Operating without registration, missing recycling targets or dealing with unregistered entities all carry significant enforcement consequences.

01
Mandatory Registration — No Exceptions
Every manufacturer, producer, importer, recycler, refurbisher and dismantler of EEE listed in Schedule I of the 2022 Rules must register on the SPCB EPR Portal. Business cannot be conducted without registration — and no entity may deal with an unregistered counterpart. Both conditions carry enforcement penalties.
02
Annual EPR Targets Are Binding
Producers are assigned annual e-waste recycling obligations based on their prior-year sales volumes. Failure to meet targets through procurement of EPR certificates from authorised recyclers attracts environmental compensation charges under the 2022 Rules — equivalent to the cost of recycling the unmet quantity.
03
RoHS Compliance Obligation
The 2022 Rules mandate that all EEE and their components must not contain lead, mercury, cadmium, hexavalent chromium, polybrominated biphenyls and PBDEs beyond prescribed maximum concentration values. Non-compliance with RoHS provisions triggers product-level liability and market access restrictions.
What we do

Complete e-waste compliance —
registration to annual target fulfilment

We manage the full spectrum of e-waste compliance obligations — from initial SPCB EPR Portal registration and GPCB authorization to annual target calculation, EPR certificate procurement and return filing.

Entity Classification & Applicability Assessment
We determine your precise classification under the 2022 Rules — producer, manufacturer, importer, recycler, refurbisher or dismantler — and identify your specific registration requirements, EPR obligations, target schedules and GPCB authorization requirements.
SPCB EPR Portal Registration
We prepare and file your complete registration application on the SPCB EPR Portal including EPR plan, brand and product details, historical sales data, target schedule submission and all supporting documentation required for SPCB's 120-day review process.
GPCB Authorization for Recyclers & Dismantlers
E-waste recycling and dismantling facilities in Gujarat require GPCB authorization in addition to SPCB registration. We manage the complete GPCB authorization application — facility inspection, storage compliance, pollution control systems and e-Gujcop filing.
Annual EPR Target Calculation & Management
We calculate your annual EPR obligation based on prior-year sales, track target fulfilment status throughout the year and procure EPR certificates from SPCB-registered recyclers to ensure targets are met before the financial year close.
Annual Compliance Filing & Reporting
We prepare and submit your annual e-waste compliance return on the SPCB EPR Portal, EPR certificate reconciliation, RoHS documentation maintenance and any GPCB annual environmental statements required for recycler authorisation renewal.
