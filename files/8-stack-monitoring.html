Why it matters

Stack monitoring is a
legal obligation — not a choice.

Every industry with a chimney, boiler, DG set, kiln or furnace above the threshold capacity is legally required to conduct periodic stack emission monitoring. Non-compliance is a criminal offence under Indian environmental law.

01
Statutory Requirement
Stack monitoring is mandated under the Environment Protection Act, 1986 and the Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1981. The CPCB and respective SPCBs (GPCB for Gujarat) prescribe monitoring frequency, parameters and reporting formats — typically quarterly or half-yearly for most industries.
02
Consent to Operate (CTO) Condition
Stack monitoring reports are a mandatory condition of every Consent to Operate issued by the SPCB. Failure to submit monitoring data on time is treated as a violation of CTO conditions and can trigger suspension or revocation of the consent — shutting operations.
03
NABL Accreditation Required
The CPCB and most SPCBs require that stack emission monitoring be conducted only by NABL-accredited laboratories. Reports from non-accredited sources are rejected for regulatory submission and do not provide legal protection in show-cause proceedings or judicial hearings.
04
Pollution Index & Category Rating
The CPCB’s Pollution Index system rates industries Red, Orange, Green or White based on emission levels and compliance history. Consistently high stack emissions push your index score upward, attracting more frequent inspections, public reporting exposure and mandatory pollution control investment orders.
What we do

End-to-end stack monitoring —
from sampling to submission

We handle every stage of the stack monitoring cycle: pre-sampling preparation, on-site isokinetic sampling and field measurement, NABL-accredited laboratory analysis, regulatory report preparation and SPCB/CPCB submission.

Pre-Survey & Stack Audit
On-site inspection of all emission points, identification of stacks requiring monitoring under your CTO conditions, measurement port location review, and confirmation of sampling access arrangements before monitoring day.
Isokinetic Sampling (IS 11255 / US EPA Method 5)
Trained field engineers conduct isokinetic particulate sampling and simultaneous gas phase sampling using calibrated equipment — ensuring sampling is conducted at the correct velocity to capture representative particulate matter concentrations.
In-Situ Gas Measurement
On-site real-time measurement of SO₂, NOₓ, CO, CO₂ and O₂ using portable analysers calibrated against reference gases, with simultaneous measurement of flue gas temperature, velocity and moisture content per CPCB protocols.
NABL-Accredited Laboratory Analysis
Collected samples are analysed at our NABL-accredited laboratory using CPCB-prescribed analytical methods — producing results legally accepted by all SPCBs, the CPCB, courts and environmental tribunals.
Regulatory Report & Submission
Complete monitoring report prepared in SPCB-prescribed format with all results, calculations, equipment calibration records, field data sheets and QA/QC documentation. We prepare your submission directly for GPCB or applicable SPCB portal upload.

FAQ

Frequently asked
questions

Monitoring frequency depends on your industry category and SPCB consent conditions. Red category industries are typically required to monitor quarterly; Orange category half-yearly; and Green category annually. However, your specific CTO conditions override these general timelines — some industries with high-impact stacks are required to monitor monthly. CEMS-fitted units have continuous data obligations. We review your CTO conditions at kickoff and establish the monitoring calendar to ensure you never miss a deadline.

Isokinetic sampling is the technique of drawing stack gas into a sampling nozzle at exactly the same velocity as the gas flowing in the duct — so that particles enter the probe without being deflected or concentrated by velocity differences. If sampling is not isokinetic (sub- or super-isokinetic), heavier particles are either over- or under-sampled, producing results that are not representative of actual emissions. Indian Standard IS 11255 and US EPA Method 5 both mandate isokinetic sampling for particulate matter measurement. Our engineers calculate and maintain isokinetic conditions in real time throughout each sampling run.

In serious or repeat cases, the SPCB can issue a closure direction.
