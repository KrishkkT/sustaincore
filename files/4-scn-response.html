Why it matters

A wrong SCN response
escalates the enforcement action.

Show-Cause Notices are the gateway to closure directions, prosecution and consent suspension. How you respond — and how fast — determines whether the matter is resolved or escalated.

01
Narrow Response Window
GPCB SCNs typically allow 15–21 days for response. Missing the deadline or filing an incomplete response is treated as non-cooperation — substantially increasing the likelihood of a closure direction or prosecution being initiated without further opportunity to be heard.
02
Technical Precision is Critical
SCN responses require accurate technical rebuttal of alleged violations — emission data, treatment plant performance, monitoring records, waste disposal documentation. Generic or vague responses that do not directly address each allegation with evidence are invariably treated adversely by GPCB.
03
Hearing Representation Matters
GPCB conducts personal hearings before issuing closure or prosecution orders. The hearing is your best — and sometimes only — opportunity to present your compliance position in person. An unprepared or unrepresented hearing leads to orders that are far harder to challenge subsequently.
04
Remediation Commitment is Expected
GPCB's enforcement approach rewards demonstrable corrective action — facilities that proactively remediate violations and file a follow-up compliance report receive far more favourable outcomes than those that only contest the notice. Combining a strong response with a concrete remediation plan is the most effective defence strategy.
Our service scope

Notice receipt to
hearing closure and remediation

We engage from the moment you receive an SCN — providing technical, documentary and representational support through every stage of the enforcement proceeding.

01
SCN Analysis & Strategy
We analyse the SCN in detail — identifying each allegation, the specific statutory provisions invoked, the evidence cited by GPCB and any procedural defects in the notice itself. We then prepare a response strategy that addresses strong allegations with evidence and challenges weak or unsupported ones on technical and legal grounds.
02
Evidence Compilation
We work with your team to compile all relevant supporting documents — monitoring records, laboratory analysis reports, equipment maintenance logs, consent conditions, waste disposal manifests, staff training records and investment evidence — organizing them into a structured response bundle that directly counters each allegation.
03
SCN Response Drafting & Filing
We draft a technically and legally precise written response to GPCB — including a point-by-point rebuttal of each allegation, supporting evidence references, an explanation of any genuine violations and a concrete corrective action plan with committed timelines. The response is filed within the stipulated period with acknowledgement retained.
04
GPCB Hearing Representation & Follow-up
We represent your facility at the GPCB personal hearing — presenting your compliance position, answering technical queries and submitting additional evidence. Post-hearing, we prepare the compliance report demonstrating remediation completion and follow up with GPCB until the matter is formally closed.
What we cover

SCN types we defend
across all violation categories

Consent Condition Violations
SCNs for exceeding emission or effluent limits, operating beyond permitted capacity, or non-compliance with consent-specific conditions.
Hazardous Waste Violations
SCNs for unauthorized storage, disposal or transfer of hazardous waste including used oil, ETP sludge, spent solvents and e-waste.
Consent Expiry & Operation Without CTO
SCNs for operating after CTO expiry, failure to renew in time, or operating facilities without a valid Consent to Establish or Operate.
Annual Return Non-filing
SCNs for non-filing or late filing of Annual Environmental Statement, hazardous waste returns or BMW annual returns.
EPR Non-compliance
CPCB and GPCB notices for failure to register under battery, plastic or e-waste EPR frameworks or failure to meet annual collection targets.
Bio-Medical Waste Violations
GPCB notices to healthcare facilities for improper BMW segregation, unauthorized disposal, missing CBWTF linkage or staff training non-compliance.
