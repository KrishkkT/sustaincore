Why it matters

A wrong SCN response
escalates the enforcement action.

Show-Cause Notices are the gateway to closure directions, prosecution and consent suspension. How you respond — and how fast — determines whether the matter is resolved or escalated.

Narrow Response Window
GPCB SCNs typically allow 15–21 days for response. Missing the deadline or filing an incomplete response is treated as non-cooperation — substantially increasing the likelihood of a closure direction or prosecution being initiated without further opportunity to be heard.
Technical Precision is Critical
SCN responses require accurate technical rebuttal of alleged violations — emission data, treatment plant performance, monitoring records, waste disposal documentation. Generic or vague responses that do not directly address each allegation with evidence are invariably treated adversely by GPCB.
Hearing Representation Matters
GPCB conducts personal hearings before issuing closure or prosecution orders. The hearing is your best — and sometimes only — opportunity to present your compliance position in person. An unprepared or unrepresented hearing leads to orders that are far harder to challenge subsequently.
Remediation Commitment is Expected
GPCB's enforcement approach rewards demonstrable corrective action — facilities that proactively remediate violations and file a follow-up compliance report receive far more favourable outcomes than those that only contest the notice.
What we do

Complete SCN management —
from audit to final closure

We manage every element of the SCN response cycle — identifying technical gaps, drafting evidence-based rebuttals, and representing your facility at GPCB hearings to secure a formal withdrawal of enforcement actions.

Notice & Compliance Review
We conduct an immediate review of the Show-Cause Notice to identify the specific rule violations alleged, the evidence cited by GPCB, and the potential enforcement impact. We then audit your facility data to build a technical rebuttal against each allegation.
Technical Rebuttal Drafting
We draft a point-by-point technical and legal response to each allegation — incorporating monitoring reports, maintenance logs, waste manifest records and any other documentary evidence that mitigates the alleged non-compliance.
GPCB Hearing Representation
We represent your unit before GPCB officers during the personal hearing — presenting the technical defence, clarifying operational realities, and demonstrating the remediation steps already taken to resolve the concerns cited in the notice.
Compliance Restoration filing
Post-remediation, we prepare and file the formal Compliance Report with GPCB, including all necessary photographic evidence and technical data, to secure the formal withdrawal of the SCN or restart directions.
