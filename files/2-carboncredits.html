Why it matters

Not all carbon credits
are equal. Quality matters.

The carbon credit market has a credibility crisis driven by poor-quality credits. We only source and retire credits that meet the highest integrity standards — because your net zero claim must withstand investor and NGO scrutiny.

01
Net Zero Credibility
SBTi’s Corporate Net Zero Standard endorses high-quality credits for residual unavoidable emissions. Without verifiable credit retirement, your net zero claim cannot be substantiated to investors, customers or regulators.
02
Regulatory Opportunity
India’s Carbon Credit Trading Scheme (CCTS) under the Energy Conservation Act creates both compliance obligations and trading opportunities for energy-intensive industries. Early movers gain a structural advantage.
03
Supply Chain Neutralisation
Global MNCs increasingly require their Indian suppliers to offset supply chain emissions under Scope 3 reduction programmes. Verified credit retirement is the accepted mechanism for demonstrating this offsetting.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Carbon credits are credible when used correctly — as a bridge for residual unavoidable emissions after deep reduction has occurred, not as a substitute for reduction. SBTi’s Corporate Net Zero Standard requires companies to halve emissions by 2030 and reach near-zero by 2050, with credits covering only residual unavoidable emissions (below 10% of baseline). The Oxford Principles provide additional guidance on credit quality. We design offset strategies that satisfy these requirements and can withstand investor and NGO scrutiny.

Gold Standard was established by WWF and focuses on renewable energy and energy efficiency projects with strong social co-benefit requirements. Verra VCS (Verified Carbon Standard) covers a broader range of project types including forestry (REDD+), agriculture and industrial. Both are internationally recognised. Gold Standard is generally considered higher credibility due to its more rigorous additionality and co-benefit requirements. We recommend Gold Standard for maximum claim defensibility and Verra VCS for specific project types not covered by Gold Standard.

India’s CCTS, established under the Energy Conservation (Amendment) Act 2022 and administered by BEE (Bureau of Energy Efficiency), is India’s domestic carbon credit mechanism. It covers energy-intensive industries under the Perform, Achieve and Trade (PAT) scheme and will expand to voluntary market participants. Companies that over-achieve their energy consumption reduction targets generate carbon credit certificates that can be traded. We help eligible organisations understand their CCTS position, maximise credit generation and navigate the trading mechanism.
