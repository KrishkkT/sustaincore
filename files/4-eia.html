Regulatory Framework

Category A vs Category B projects

EIA Notification 2006 divides projects into two categories based on scale and environmental sensitivity. The category determines which authority appraises your project and the level of scrutiny applied.

Category A
Central Appraisal — MoEFCC
  • Appraised by Expert Appraisal Committee (EAC) at MoEFCC, New Delhi
  • Larger projects: refinery, thermal power >500 MW, mining >150 ha, large ports
  • Standard TOR granted centrally by MoEFCC
  • Public hearing mandatory in all cases
  • EC issued by MoEFCC — typically 12–18 months total timeline
  • Post-EC compliance reports submitted to MoEFCC Regional Office
Category B
State Appraisal — SEIAA Gujarat
  • Appraised by SEAC; EC issued by SEIAA Gujarat
  • Mid-scale projects: industries, infrastructure, mining <150 ha, hotels
  • B1: mandatory public hearing  |  B2: no public hearing
  • TOR issued by SEIAA/SEAC on application
  • EC timeline: typically 9–14 months
  • Post-EC reports to SEIAA Gujarat and GPCB
Our Process

The EIA process — stage by stage

Each phase is sequential and mandatory. Delays at any stage push the entire timeline. Our project management keeps every phase on track.

01
Screening & TOR Application
2–4 Weeks

Project categorisation (A or B) confirmed against EIA Notification 2006 Schedule. Form 1 / Form 1A and pre-feasibility report prepared and submitted to MoEFCC or SEIAA Gujarat via the Parivesh portal. We draft the TOR application with a detailed project description and proposed scope of baseline studies to accelerate TOR grant — which can take 30–60 days from the authority.

Form 1 / 1A Pre-feasibility Report Parivesh Submission TOR Draft
02
Baseline Data Collection
3–6 Months

Systematic collection of baseline environmental data across the study area (typically 10 km radius) across a minimum of one season (three seasons preferred for robust assessment). We coordinate NABL-accredited laboratories for air quality, water quality (surface and groundwater), soil, noise and ecology surveys, and organise socio-economic baseline through structured community surveys.

Ambient Air Quality Water & Soil Sampling Ecology Survey Noise Monitoring Socio-economic Survey Meteorological Data
03
EIA Report Preparation
2–3 Months

Preparation of the comprehensive EIA report by our NABET-linked environmental experts. The report includes full project description, baseline characterisation, prediction and evaluation of environmental impacts using quantitative models (air dispersion modelling, groundwater modelling where applicable), alternatives analysis, cumulative impact assessment, risk assessment and a detailed Environmental Management Plan (EMP) with budget.

EIA Report Air Dispersion Model Impact Matrix Risk Assessment EMP with Budget Executive Summary
04
Public Consultation
30–60 Days

For Category A and B1 projects, public hearing is conducted by the State Pollution Control Board after a mandatory 30-day public notice. We prepare the non-technical summary in local languages (Gujarati/Hindi), coordinate with GPCB for the hearing schedule, prepare project representatives for community questions, and compile the response-to-comments document that must be submitted to the appraisal authority.

Non-technical Summary Gujarati Translation Hearing Attendance Response to Comments Public Hearing Minutes
05
Appraisal & EC Grant
60–90 Days

EIA report, public hearing proceedings and supporting documents submitted to EAC (Category A) or SEAC Gujarat (Category B) via Parivesh portal. We represent the project at appraisal committee presentations, prepare responses to EAC/SEAC queries and compile additional data requests. Environmental Clearance is typically granted within 60 days of appraisal, subject to condition setting.

Parivesh Submission Appraisal Presentation Query Responses EC Order
06
Post-EC Compliance
Ongoing

EC conditions impose binding obligations throughout the project lifecycle — six-monthly compliance reports to MoEFCC/SEIAA, environmental monitoring, afforestation, community development spending and display board installation. We set up a compliance management system to track every EC condition and prepare the mandatory six-monthly reports on schedule.

EIA Report Structure

What goes into an appraiser-ready EIA

A weak EIA report draws prolonged EAC/SEAC queries, delays appraisal and risks rejection. Every component below is rigorously prepared against MoEFCC guidelines.

Chapter 01
Project Description
Process description, raw material and utility requirements, land use, infrastructure layout, construction methodology and project phasing — with site maps and layout plans.
Chapter 02
Baseline Environmental Status
Season-specific data on air quality, water quality, soil, noise, ecology, land use and socio-economic conditions within the 10 km study area. NABL lab reports attached.
Chapter 03
Alternatives Analysis
Site alternatives, technology alternatives and no-project alternative assessed against environmental and socio-economic criteria — a mandatory but often weak chapter in inadequate EIAs.
Chapter 04
Impact Prediction & Evaluation
Quantitative impact prediction using AERMOD/CALPUFF dispersion models for air, groundwater models, noise propagation models and ecology impact matrices. Significance determination per standard criteria.
Chapter 05
Environmental Management Plan
Theme-wise mitigation measures for every significant impact — with budget, responsibility matrix, implementation schedule and performance indicators. The EMP is a binding commitment post-EC.
Chapter 06
Risk Assessment & DMP
Quantitative risk assessment (HAZOP, fault tree, consequence modelling) for chemical/industrial projects. Disaster Management Plan covering prevention, preparedness, response and recovery.
Chapter 07
Corporate Environment Policy
Formal environmental commitment document from the organisation's board — evaluated by EAC/SEAC as evidence of management system maturity and long-term environmental commitment.
Chapter 08
Environmental Monitoring Plan
Post-EC monitoring programme specifying parameters, frequency, sampling locations, responsible agency and reporting mechanism — covering construction and operational phases.
Chapter 09
Summary & Conclusions
Non-technical executive summary and Gujarati/Hindi summary for public consultation. Benefit-cost analysis and statement of residual impacts after mitigation.

Frequently asked questions

Site preparation, earthwork, civil construction or any physical activity before EC constitutes an offence under the Environment Protection Act 1986, attracting closure, penalty and prosecution. For Category B projects (SEIAA Gujarat), the realistic timeline from TOR application to EC grant is 9–14 months — comprising TOR grant (1–2 months), baseline data collection (3–6 months, at least one complete season), EIA preparation (2–3 months), public hearing (1–2 months) and appraisal (1–3 months). Category A projects at MoEFCC typically take 12–18 months due to EAC appraisal queues. Proactive, complete submissions significantly reduce appraisal rounds and delays.

Yes. MoEFCC mandates that EIA studies be conducted by NABET-QCI (National Accreditation Board for Education and Training — Quality Council of India) accredited consultant organisations. Accreditation is sector-specific — a consultant accredited for industrial projects may not be accredited for mining. We work with NABET-accredited partner organisations for the specific sectors your project falls under, ensuring your EIA report is compliant and accepted by EAC/SEAC without procedural challenge.

Public hearing objections do not automatically block EC. The EAC/SEAC is required to consider public hearing minutes but is not bound by the volume of objections. What matters is how the project proponent responds to concerns raised — factually, technically and with specific mitigation commitments. We prepare thorough written responses to every concern raised at the public hearing, and our pre-hearing community engagement strategy minimises adversarial situations before they arise.

Expansion projects that trigger the Schedule thresholds of EIA Notification 2006 — by increasing production capacity, adding new products or expanding the project area — require a fresh EC (or EC amendment in some cases). The requirement applies even if the original project had EC. We assess your specific expansion parameters against the EIA Notification Schedule and advise whether a fresh EC, EC amendment or no fresh EC is required before you commit to expansion timelines.
