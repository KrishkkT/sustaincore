The two stages of consent

From groundbreaking to
production launch

Any industry project requires two distinct stages of environmental consent before a commercial product can leave the gate.

01
Consent to Establish (CTE)

The "Intent-to-Build" license required before any construction begins on site. Covers site suitability and planned pollution control systems.

  • Site plan & layout review
  • Planned emission / effluent estimates
  • ETP / STP / APCM technical drawings
  • SPCB portal application management
02
Consent to Operate (CTO)

The "License-to-Run" required before start of commercial production. Verified that all systems are installed as per CTE conditions.

  • Post-installation technical audit
  • Performance evaluation of ETP / APCM
  • Consent renewal & amendment filing
  • Compliance to SPCB conditions
Why it's critical

Without Consent, there is no
lawful industrial activity.

The Air (1981) and Water (1974) Acts provide SPCB with the power of closure and disconnection of electricity/water for non-compliance.

01
Commercial Closure Risk
Operating without a valid CTO is a "cognizable offense" that can lead to immediate closure notices and sealing of units by the Regional Office of the SPCB.
02
Electricity Disconnection
SPCBs have statutory power to direct DISCOMs to disconnect power supply to units operating without consent or violating consent conditions.
03
Penalty & Compensation
Non-compliance flags can lead to retrospective Environmental Compensation (EC) charges that can financially cripple mid-sized industrial units.
