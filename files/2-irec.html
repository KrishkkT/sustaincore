Why it matters

Renewable energy claims
require traceable evidence.

Under the GHG Protocol’s market-based accounting method, your Scope 2 renewable electricity claim is only valid if backed by a formally retired energy attribute certificate. An I-REC is that evidence.

CDP Market-based Scope 2
CDP requires companies disclosing Scope 2 to report both location-based and market-based emissions. Market-based requires contractual instruments — PPAs, green tariffs or energy attribute certificates. I-RECs are the recognised instrument for India.
GHG Protocol Compliance
The GHG Protocol Scope 2 Guidance requires energy attribute certificates that meet specific quality criteria: issued for the same reporting period, retired exclusively on behalf of the company, and from a system that prevents double-counting. I-RECs satisfy all criteria.
Supply Chain RE100 Alignment
Companies that are RE100 members or that supply RE100 companies must demonstrate 100% renewable electricity through verified instruments. I-RECs are accepted by RE100 as a qualifying instrument for markets where direct PPAs are not available.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Location-based Scope 2 uses the average emission factor of the electricity grid you are connected to — it reflects the carbon intensity of the physical grid. Market-based Scope 2 uses contractual instruments — PPAs, green tariffs, or energy attribute certificates like I-RECs — to reflect the specific electricity source your organisation has contracted. If you have retired I-RECs for renewable electricity you have purchased or generated, your market-based Scope 2 can be zero even if the grid is coal-heavy. CDP requires both to be reported; most investors focus on market-based.

No — they are different instruments. An I-REC (energy attribute certificate) documents the renewable origin of 1 MWh of electricity — it addresses Scope 2 electricity emissions by proving your electricity came from a clean source. A carbon credit documents the avoidance or removal of 1 tonne of CO2 equivalent — it is used to offset emissions that have already occurred. They have different registries, standards and accounting treatments. I-RECs reduce your market-based Scope 2 to zero for the electricity covered; carbon credits reduce your residual emissions after you have achieved deep reduction.

If you own or operate on-site solar generation, you can use that electricity as market-based Scope 2 without I-RECs — provided you retain the attribute (i.e., do not sell or transfer the certificate rights). If you sell the electricity or certificates, you must purchase I-RECs separately to make a market-based claim. For off-site renewable energy — which accounts for most corporate renewable electricity — I-RECs or PPAs are the required instrument. We help you design the most cost-effective combination of on-site generation and I-REC procurement for your Scope 2 target.
