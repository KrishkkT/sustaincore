Understanding the standard

What ISO 45001requires

ISO 45001:2018 uses the Annex SL high-level structure, enabling integration with ISO 14001 and ISO 9001. It emphasises leadership commitment, worker participation, hazard identification, and risk-based thinking to prevent harm before it occurs.

Why it matters

Business case forISO 45001

Beyond moral obligation, effective OH&S management delivers measurable commercial benefits and reduces operational risk.

01

Legal Compliance

A certified OHSMS provides a structured framework to identify applicable health and safety legislation, track compliance obligations, and demonstrate due diligence. This reduces the risk of penalties, prosecutions, and directors' personal liability under occupational safety laws.

02

Incident Cost Reduction

Workplace injuries carry direct costs (medical expenses, compensation, fines) and indirect costs (lost productivity, investigation time, equipment damage, reputational impact). Organisations with mature OHSMS typically achieve 50-60% reduction in recordable incidents.

03

Supply Chain Access

Global buyers increasingly require ISO 45001 certification as part of supplier qualification — particularly in construction, manufacturing, oil & gas, and mining sectors. EcoVadis Labour & Human Rights scoring heavily weights OH&S management system evidence.

04

Insurance Benefits

Many insurers offer reduced premiums on workers' compensation and liability policies to organisations with certified OHSMS — recognising reduced claim frequency and severity. The investment in certification often pays back through insurance savings alone.

05

Employee Engagement

ISO 45001's emphasis on worker participation and consultation improves safety culture. Employees who feel their safety is prioritised show higher engagement, lower turnover, and greater productivity. A safe workplace attracts and retains talent.

06

ESG Performance

Health and safety metrics are core Social indicators in ESG frameworks. ISO 45001 certification provides documented evidence for GRI 403, BRSR Principle 3, and EcoVadis Labour & Human Rights assessments — demonstrating responsible employment practices to investors.

FAQ

Frequently askedquestions

ISO 45001:2018 replaced OHSAS 18001 with several significant changes: adoption of Annex SL structure for integration with other ISO standards, explicit requirement for top management leadership and accountability, emphasis on worker participation and consultation, risk-based thinking approach, requirements for managing risks and opportunities (not just hazards), and focus on organisational context. OHSAS 18001 certificates expired in March 2021 — organisations must now certify to ISO 45001.

For most organisations, the timeline from project kickoff to certification is 4-6 months. This depends on organisational size, complexity of operations, existing safety maturity, and resource availability. Organisations with previous OHSAS 18001 certification or existing safety management practices typically achieve certification faster. High-hazard operations requiring extensive HIRA development may require additional time.

ISO 45001 requires organisations to establish processes for consultation and participation of workers at all levels. This includes involvement in hazard identification and risk assessment, determination of controls, incident investigation, development of OH&S policy and objectives, and decisions about monitoring and measurement. Worker participation mechanisms must be documented and workers must have access to relevant OH&S information.

Yes — ISO 45001 and ISO 14001 both use the Annex SL high-level structure, making integration straightforward. Many requirements are identical or parallel: context of the organisation, leadership, planning, support, performance evaluation, and improvement. We design OHSMS documentation to align with existing EMS structure, enabling combined audits and reducing overall management system burden.

ISO 45001 certification is one of the most efficient ways to improve your EcoVadis Labour & Human Rights theme score. The certificate demonstrates a mature OH&S management system — weighted evidence in EcoVadis scoring. Beyond the certificate, ISO 45001 generates the policies, procedures, incident data, and performance metrics that EcoVadis assessors evaluate. Organisations with ISO 45001 typically score significantly higher on Labour than those relying solely on policy statements.
