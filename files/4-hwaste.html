Hazardous Waste Authorization

Any industry in Gujarat that generates, stores, transports or disposes of hazardous waste listed under the Hazardous and Other Wastes (Management & Transboundary Movement) Rules 2016 must obtain a separate GPCB authorization — regardless of quantity. Common generators include chemical, pharmaceutical, textile, electroplating, paint, printing, ETP sludge and used-oil industries. We manage the complete HW authorization lifecycle.

Why it matters

Hazardous waste without authorisation
is a strict liability offence.

Hazardous waste rules carry no minimum quantity threshold. Any industry generating a Schedule I, II or III waste — even in small quantities — requires GPCB authorization. The penalties are severe and personal.

No Minimum Quantity Exemption
Unlike some environmental regulations, HW Rules 2016 do not provide a de minimis exemption. A unit generating even a few kilograms of spent solvent, ETP sludge, used oil or electroplating waste requires formal authorization — the absence of which exposes the company to enforcement action.
Storage Time Limits
Hazardous waste may not be stored on-site for more than 90 days without GPCB approval for an extension. Unauthorized accumulation of HW is treated as illegal dumping under the Rules and can attract NGT orders, criminal proceedings and liability for environmental remediation costs.
Manifest System Compliance
All hazardous waste transported in Gujarat must be covered by a valid GPCB manifest. Transportation without manifest — or by an unauthorised transporter — creates joint liability for the generator. GPCB's GPS-based Vehicle Location Tracking System (VLTS) monitors all HW vehicles in real-time.
What we do

Complete HW authorization —
from waste audit to TSDF linkage

We manage every element of the Hazardous Waste authorization process — identifying applicable waste schedules, preparing compliant storage facilities, obtaining GPCB authorization and establishing the manifest and TSDF disposal chain.

Hazardous Waste Stream Audit
We conduct a comprehensive audit of your manufacturing processes to identify all waste streams, classify them against Schedule I, II and III of HW Rules 2016, determine applicable category codes and quantify annual generation volumes.
Storage Facility Compliance Assessment
We assess your existing HW storage area against GPCB requirements — segregation, labelling, containment, fire safety, access control, weather protection — and provide a gap-remediation plan to bring storage into full compliance before application.
Authorization Application Preparation
We prepare the complete HW authorization application under Rule 9 of HW Rules 2016, including waste characterisation data, storage facility details, transportation plan, disposal route and authorised TSDF/CHWIF linkages — in GPCB-prescribed format.
TSDF & Authorised Transporter Linkage
We identify the most suitable GPCB-authorised Treatment, Storage & Disposal Facility (TSDF) for your waste streams and arrange tie-ups with authorised HW transporters operating under GPCB's VLTS tracking system.
Ongoing Annual Return & Manifest Filing
Post-authorization, we manage the annual HW return filing to GPCB, manifest generation and reconciliation, TSDF disposal certificates and the passbook compliance tracking system maintained by GPCB's Hazardous Waste Branch.

Frequently asked questions

Yes. Hazardous Waste Rules 2016 do not prescribe a minimum quantity threshold for authorization. Any unit generating waste listed in Schedule I, II or III of the Rules requires authorization. Common examples include spent solvents, ETP sludge, used oils, electroplating waste, paint sludge, pharmaceutical process residues and discarded chemical containers. We first conduct a waste stream audit to identify all applicable schedules before preparing the authorization application.

Under HW Rules 2016, Schedule I lists wastes with specific hazardous constituents (e.g. lead, mercury, cadmium compounds, cyanides, spent chlorinated solvents). Schedule II lists wastes that are hazardous based on their concentration or characteristics (ignitability, corrosivity, reactivity, toxicity). Schedule III lists wastes subject to transboundary movement restrictions. A single industry may generate wastes falling under multiple schedules — each requiring classification and inclusion in the authorization application.

The general storage limit is 90 days. Rule 8(v) of HW Rules 2016 permits GPCB to extend this period on application with sufficient justification — typically when disposal infrastructure is unavailable or TSDF capacity is constrained. We assist in filing for storage period extensions where required, documenting the operational reasons and ensuring compliance with GPCB's conditions for the extension, including secure storage maintenance and manifest trail for all stored quantities.

GPCB has implemented a GPS-based Vehicle Location Tracking System (VLTS) under HW Rules 2016 for all vehicles transporting hazardous waste in Gujarat. All authorised HW transporters must operate vehicles equipped with GPCB-approved GPS devices, and all HW movements are tracked in real-time via the VLTS portal. As a generator, you must ensure that your contracted transporter is GPCB-authorised and VLTS-compliant, and that every transport movement is covered by a valid manifest linked to the transport record in the VLTS system.

GPCB's Consolidated Consent & Authorisation (CCA) format combines the operating consent under Air and Water Acts with the Hazardous Waste Authorization under HW Rules 2016 into a single document for most industries. However, this integration depends on whether the HW authorization was included at the time of the original CTO application. In some cases — particularly for industries that began generating new waste streams after the CTO was issued — a separate HW authorization may be required. We assess your specific situation and file the appropriate application.
