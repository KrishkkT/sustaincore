Frequently askedquestions

CDP scores responses from D (disclosed) to A (leadership), with D-, C, B and A- in between. Scoring is based on the depth, quality and rigour of responses across four themes: disclosure completeness, awareness of environmental risks, management quality, and leadership (best practices, verification, targets, value chain engagement). Each module (Climate, Water, Forest) is scored independently. The full scoring methodology is updated annually and we track all changes to optimise your score.

The CDP A-list recognises companies demonstrating best practice across all four scoring themes â€” including verified emissions data, board-level climate governance, science-based targets, and active supplier engagement. Globally, only around 2-3% of disclosing companies achieve A-list status. We design every CDP engagement with A-list criteria as the target, even when the current year target is B or A-.

CDP does not require third-party verification but awards additional scoring credit for it, particularly in the Management and Leadership bands. We recommend limited-scope GHG verification (ISO 14064-3) as a cost-effective way to access this scoring uplift. Verification also strengthens your position with institutional investors and satisfies CDP customer programmes that require verified emissions data from supply chain members.

CDP Climate module questions map directly onto TCFDâ€™s four pillars â€” governance, strategy, risk management, and metrics & targets. CDP is recognised by the Financial Stability Board as a leading TCFD implementation mechanism. Completing a rigorous CDP Climate response therefore simultaneously satisfies TCFD disclosure requirements, reducing duplication and ensuring both disclosures are internally consistent. We manage this integration as part of every CDP engagement.
