Why it matters

AES filing gaps create
CTO renewal complications.

The Annual Environmental Statement is GPCB's primary tool for tracking a unit's environmental performance over time. Missing filings are among the most common deficiencies flagged during CTO renewal scrutiny.

CTO Renewal Pre-condition
GPCB verifies AES filing history as part of CTO renewal scrutiny. Missing or late AES returns — even for years prior to the renewal period — create queries, delays and potentially adverse renewal conditions. Many companies discover historical AES gaps only when their renewal is held up.
Direct Legal Obligation
Rule 14 of the Environment Protection Rules, 1986 imposes a direct legal obligation to submit Form V. Non-filing is an independent compliance violation — separate from consent conditions — and may attract prosecution under Section 15 of the EPA regardless of whether your consent is otherwise current.
Inspection Trigger
GPCB's inspection scheduling system flags units with missing or consistently late AES filings for priority site visits. An AES filing gap is effectively a signal to GPCB that a unit's environmental monitoring and recordkeeping practices need verification.
Data Accuracy Matters
The AES requires specific data on water consumption, wastewater generation, air emission loads, solid and hazardous waste quantities and environmental expenditure. Inaccurate data — understating generation or overstating treatment — is treated as misrepresentation during audits and consent renewals.

Form V requires data across six key areas: (1) water consumption by source and use — ground, surface, municipal; (2) wastewater generated, treated and discharged; (3) air emissions from stacks with pollutant loads; (4) solid waste generated, reused, recycled and disposed; (5) hazardous waste generated and disposed of with authorization details; (6) environmental capital expenditure and revenue expenditure for the year.
