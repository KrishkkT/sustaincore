Why it matters

Customers are asking
what is in your product’s footprint.

EU customers are under legal obligation to account for Scope 3 emissions in their supply chain. Your product’s carbon footprint is their Category 1 data. Without a PCF, you make them estimate — and estimates are usually pessimistic.

01
Scope 3 Customer Demand
Your EU, US and Japanese customers need primary PCF data from suppliers to manage their Scope 3 Category 1 (purchased goods and services). A verified PCF replaces their rough estimates — and typically shows lower emissions, reducing their reported Scope 3 burden.
02
CBAM Alignment
CBAM requires embedded emission intensity per tonne of exported product — which is essentially a PCF. An ISO 14067 PCF provides the methodological rigour that EU importers can use for CBAM compliance with greater credibility than CBAM default values.
03
Carbon Labelling
Consumer and B2B markets are moving toward carbon-labelled products in food, packaging, construction and consumer electronics. A credible PCF is the prerequisite for any carbon label or “low carbon” product claim.
What we do

ISO 14067 PCF development —
from LCA to verified carbon label

We build Product Carbon Footprints that satisfy ISO 14067, GHG Protocol Product Standard, EU customer requirements and CBAM methodology simultaneously.

System Boundary & Scope Definition
We define the functional unit, system boundary (cradle-to-gate, cradle-to-grave or cradle-to-cradle) and cut-off rules per ISO 14067 and GHG Protocol Product Standard — aligned to your customer’s specific requirements.
Primary Data Collection
We collect primary production data from your facility — raw material inputs, energy consumption, process emissions, transport, waste and water — through structured questionnaires and ERP data extraction.
Background Data & Emission Factors
Primary data is supplemented by secondary emission factors from peer-reviewed databases — ecoinvent, GaBi, DEFRA — with explicit documentation of each factor source, version and applicability.
Hotspot Identification
We identify the emission hotspots in your product lifecycle — typically raw materials and energy for manufacturers — providing the intelligence needed to target reduction investments with the highest carbon impact per investment.
Third-party Critical Review
PCFs require critical review by an independent LCA expert. We coordinate the review, respond to reviewer queries and manage revision through to a signed critical review statement that validates your PCF’s credibility.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

A Life Cycle Assessment (LCA) per ISO 14040/44 assesses multiple environmental impact categories across a product’s lifecycle: global warming potential, ozone depletion, eutrophication, acidification, resource depletion and more. A Product Carbon Footprint (PCF) per ISO 14067 is specifically scoped to climate impact only — it is essentially an LCA restricted to the greenhouse gas impact category. An EPD uses an LCA as its technical foundation and reports all impact categories including the PCF. For most commercial purposes, customers want the PCF (carbon data); for LEED/BREEAM compliance, they need the full EPD.

ISO 14067 requires explicit uncertainty analysis — quantifying how uncertain your result is. At first assessment using primary production data supplemented by secondary databases (ecoinvent, DEFRA), typical uncertainty is ±10-15%, which is sufficient for disclosure and benchmarking. Accuracy improves each cycle as primary supplier data replaces secondary database estimates. For CBAM purposes, your PCF’s embedded emission intensity needs to be defensible in an EU audit — we design data collection to meet this standard from the outset.

PCFs are calculated per product category — each distinct product or product group with a materially different production process or raw material mix requires a separate PCF. For manufacturers with large product ranges, we recommend starting with your highest-volume EU-exported products (CBAM priority), your most carbon-intensive products (highest reduction opportunity) and your flagship products for marketing use. We typically develop a multi-product PCF programme in phases, starting with the highest-impact subset.
