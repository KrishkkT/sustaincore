Mapping your waste to the
2016 Regulatory Schedule

Authorisation starts with correct characterization of the waste streams generating from your processes, as defined under Schedule 1 and Schedule 2 of the Rules.

Schedule 1: Process Waste
A process-based list (e.g., spent solvents from labs, ETP sludge, used oil) that automatically classifies a waste stream as "hazardous" if it matches the process.
Schedule 2: Concentration Base
Determined by technical chemical analysis. If specific contaminants (like heavy metals) exceed the set mg/kg threshold, the waste must be managed as hazardous.
Rule 9 Utilization
Special permission required when an industry wants to "reuse" or "utilize" hazardous waste from another process as a resource, bypassing landfill disposal.
The penalty of non-compliance

Improper storage & disposal is a
criminal offense under the Act.

The MoEF&CC and CPCB have established strict "Environmental Compensation" regimes specifically for the improper handling of hazardous wastes.

01
Manifest shortfalls
Every movement of hazardous waste must be tracked via a digital (online SPCB portal) or physical Manifest (Form 10). Missing or fake manifests trigger immediate show-cause notices.
02
Storage Time Limits
Industries are permitted to store hazardous waste for a maximum of 90 days. Exceeding this period without board permission results in penalties and CTO violation.
03
Illegal Disposal Notices
Dumping of hazardous waste in non-authorized landfills or selling it to non-authorized recyclers is a major violation that can lead to permanent facility closure.
