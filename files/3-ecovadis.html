Why it matters

Your EcoVadis score
directly affects your contracts.

Global procurement teams use EcoVadis as a mandatory supplier qualification gate. A score below Silver increasingly means exclusion from tender shortlists — regardless of price or quality.

Procurement Gate Requirement
Over 100,000 buyer companies globally require EcoVadis as part of supplier qualification. A score below their threshold — typically Silver — results in disqualification from procurement processes regardless of pricing or quality.
CBAM & CSRD Alignment
EU buyers facing CSRD Scope 3 reporting obligations are increasingly using EcoVadis Gold and Platinum status as a proxy for supplier emissions data quality. A high EcoVadis score directly reduces your EU customer’s ESG compliance burden.
Financial Incentives
Several major banks and financial institutions in India and Europe now offer preferential rates on supply chain finance, working capital facilities and green bonds to suppliers with EcoVadis Silver or above. Gold and Platinum unlock premium tier pricing.
Competitive Differentiation
In commodity sectors where price competition is intense, an EcoVadis Gold or Platinum medal is a genuine differentiator that justifies price premiums and secures preferred supplier relationships with sustainability-committed buyers.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

EcoVadis medals are awarded based on percentile rank within your industry, size and geography category — not absolute score alone. In practice: Silver requires approximately the 45th–54th percentile (roughly a score of 45–54 out of 100), Gold requires the top 25% (score approximately 55–74) and Platinum requires the top 1% (score approximately 75+). Exact thresholds vary slightly by category. What this means practically is that the same absolute score can achieve different medals depending on your sector’s average performance — which is why our pre-submission gap analysis benchmarks against your specific EcoVadis peer group, not a generic score target.

Preparing a strong first EcoVadis submission typically takes 8–12 weeks from kickoff to submission, depending on how much documentation already exists in your organisation. EcoVadis then takes 4–6 weeks after submission to issue your scorecard. Our preparation phase covers gap analysis (1–2 weeks), policy development (3–4 weeks), evidence compilation (2–3 weeks) and questionnaire completion (1–2 weeks). For re-assessments where policies are already in place, preparation time typically reduces to 4–6 weeks focused on updating evidence and targeting scoring improvements from the previous cycle.

EcoVadis scorecards are valid for 12 months from the date of issue. After 12 months, your scorecard expires and you must complete a new assessment to maintain your medal status and share it with buyers. Many buyers with EcoVadis procurement requirements will delist suppliers whose scorecard has lapsed. We track your renewal window and begin re-assessment preparation 3 months before expiry to ensure there is no gap in your medal status — particularly important for suppliers to buyers who conduct real-time scorecard validity checks.

EcoVadis weights the four themes differently based on your industry’s risk profile. Environment typically carries 30–35% weight for manufacturing and industrial companies, Labour & Human Rights 25–30%, Ethics 15–20% and Sustainable Procurement 10–15%. However, for high-risk procurement categories — such as textiles, electronics and chemicals — Labour & Human Rights can be weighted as heavily as Environment. Our gap analysis identifies your specific weighting profile so we can prioritise effort where it produces the largest score improvement. Improving your weakest theme by 10 points typically yields far more total score improvement than marginal gains on your strongest theme.

Yes — third-party certifications like ISO 14001, ISO 50001, SA8000, ISO 45001 and OHSAS 18001 carry significant weight in EcoVadis scoring and are among the most efficient ways to improve specific theme scores. An ISO 14001 certificate can boost your Environment theme substantially; SA8000 does the same for Labour. However, certifications alone are not sufficient for Gold or Platinum — EcoVadis also evaluates your policies, KPI data, action plans and supplier management practices. We develop a certification–policy–data triangle for each theme to maximise your total score, not just rely on certificates.
