Why it matters

Plastic waste EPR compliance
is now actively enforced.

The SPCB has moved from awareness to enforcement — issuing show-cause notices and environmental compensation demands to non-registered PIBOs. Here is what every brand and importer must understand.

Dual Registration Obligation
PIBOs must register on both the SPCB central EPR portal and with the relevant State Pollution Control Board (GPCB for Gujarat). Missing either registration creates compliance gaps — both regulators are now cross-verifying databases.
Category-wise EPR Targets
Each of the four plastic packaging categories — rigid, flexible mono-layer, flexible multi-layer, and compostable — carries separate, escalating annual collection targets. Brands using multiple packaging types must track targets independently for each category.
Environmental Compensation
The 2022 amendment introduced a mandatory Environmental Compensation payment for EPR shortfalls — computed at a per-tonne rate for unmet targets. This is not a discretionary penalty but a statutory payment that accumulates year on year.
Supply Chain Pressure
Large FMCG companies and e-commerce platforms are now requiring EPR compliance documentation from their packaging suppliers as part of procurement due diligence. Non-compliance risks vendor de-listing, not just regulatory action.

Frequently asked questions

Yes. The Plastic Waste Management Rules do not provide a turnover or volume threshold exemption for EPR registration. Any producer, importer or brand owner that places products in plastic packaging in the market — regardless of size — is obligated to register. Micro and small enterprises may have different target thresholds but still require registration. SPCB has clarified that ignorance of the obligation is not a defence.

The 2022 EPR framework categorizes plastic packaging as: Category I — rigid plastic packaging; Category II — flexible plastic packaging (mono-layer); Category III — flexible plastic packaging (multi-layer, multi-material); Category IV — compostable and bio-degradable plastic packaging. Each category has separate EPR targets and recycling routes. Most FMCG companies use multiple categories simultaneously, requiring separate target tracking per category.

Environmental Compensation (EC) is a statutory payment levied by SPCB for shortfall in meeting EPR collection targets. It is computed on the basis of metric tonnes of plastic packaging for which targets were not met — at rates specified by SPCB. EC payments go into a dedicated fund managed by SPCB and are separate from any penalties under the Environment Protection Act. Unlike penalties, EC applies automatically for shortfalls and cannot be waived — making target fulfilment financially critical.

Yes — SPCB-registered Producer Responsibility Organisations (PROs) are a recognized compliance route. A PRO collects and processes plastic waste on your behalf and issues EPR certificates that you upload against your targets on the SPCB portal. We vet PROs for SPCB registration validity, geographic coverage matching your market geography, and certificate authenticity — ensuring the certificates will be accepted by SPCB during return verification.

The SPCB registration covers your overall EPR obligation, while GPCB registration is a state-level authorization that covers plastic waste compliance within Gujarat's jurisdiction. Both carry separate deadlines and document requirements. We manage both registrations concurrently to avoid any gap between central and state compliance.
