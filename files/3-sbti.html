What SBTi covers

Scope 1, 2 and 3 —
the full emissions picture

SBTi requires setting targets across all three emission scopes. We build your GHG inventory and target framework covering every source material to your footprint.

Scope 1
Direct emissions
Emissions from sources owned or controlled by the company — the most directly actionable scope for most industrial businesses.
  • Fuel combustion in boilers and furnaces
  • Company-owned vehicle fleet
  • Process emissions (cement, chemicals)
  • Fugitive emissions from refrigerants
  • On-site power generation
Scope 2
Purchased energy
Indirect emissions from the generation of purchased electricity, steam, heat or cooling consumed by the company.
  • Purchased grid electricity
  • Purchased steam or heat
  • RE100 and PPA strategy impact
  • Market-based vs location-based accounting
  • Renewable energy certificates (RECs)
Scope 3
Value chain emissions
All other indirect emissions in the company’s value chain, both upstream (suppliers) and downstream (customers and product use).
  • Purchased goods and services (Cat. 1)
  • Business travel and commuting
  • Downstream product use and end-of-life
  • Investments and financed emissions
  • Upstream and downstream transportation
Why it matters

SBTi is the global credibility
standard for net-zero claims.

Without SBTi validation, your net-zero or carbon neutral claims are vulnerable to greenwashing accusations. With it, you have the world’s most credible independent climate commitment — recognised by investors, buyers and regulators.

Investor Requirement
CDP A-List status, MSCI ESG ratings, Sustainalytics and institutional investor ESG screens all evaluate SBTi commitment and validation status. Companies without SBTi targets face increasing cost-of-capital disadvantages as climate-oriented capital allocation accelerates.
Supply Chain Pressure
Global buyers with their own SBTi commitments are setting Scope 3 supplier engagement requirements — asking or requiring key suppliers to set their own SBTi-aligned targets. Not having targets means being a “high-emission supplier” in their Scope 3 inventory.
Greenwashing Protection
SBTi validation independently verifies that your climate targets are genuinely aligned with limiting warming to 1.5°C — protecting you from the growing regulatory and reputational risk of greenwashing allegations against unverified net-zero claims.
BRSR & ESG Disclosure Anchor
BRSR Principle 6 on environmental responsibility and climate risk disclosure is substantially strengthened by SBTi-validated targets. It also anchors TCFD climate scenario analysis and CDP climate module responses, reducing total ESG reporting effort significantly.
SBTi target types

Near-term, long-term
and net-zero targets

SBTi requires near-term targets (5–10 year horizon) and encourages long-term and net-zero targets for full science-alignment. We set all three in a single integrated framework.

Near-Term Targets
5–10 year horizon — 2030 typical
Scope 1+2: minimum 42% absolute reduction by 2030 from base year (1.5°C pathway). Scope 3: 25% absolute or 42% intensity reduction if Scope 3 >40% of total footprint. Must be submitted within 24 months of commitment.
Long-Term Targets
Net-zero by 2050 or earlier
Scope 1+2: 90% absolute reduction by 2050. Scope 3: 90% absolute reduction. Residual emissions (max 10%) addressed through permanent carbon removal — not offset credits. Validated under SBTi Corporate Net-Zero Standard.
SME Fast-Track
Streamlined pathway for SMEs
SBTi SME target-setting tool allows smaller companies to commit to a standardised 1.5°C target without full sector-specific pathway modelling — significantly reducing time and cost to commitment and validation.
What we do

From GHG inventory
to validated SBTi targets

We manage the complete SBTi journey — GHG inventory, target modelling, commitment letter, target submission and post-validation progress reporting — so you reach SBTi validation with minimal internal burden.

GHG Inventory (Scope 1, 2 & 3)
Full GHG Protocol-compliant inventory covering all material Scope 1, 2 and 3 categories. We identify the base year, collect activity data and apply appropriate emission factors — producing the audit-ready inventory that SBTi requires as the foundation for target-setting.
Materiality Screening & Sector Pathway Selection
Screening of all 15 Scope 3 categories for materiality, identification of your applicable SBTi sector guidance (steel, cement, apparel, ICT, buildings, transport etc.) and selection of the appropriate sector-specific decarbonisation pathway.
Target Modelling & Ambition Setting
Modelling of near-term and long-term target options using SBTi-approved methods (Absolute Contraction Approach, Sectoral Decarbonisation Approach, Economic Intensity Contraction). We identify the most achievable targets that still meet 1.5°C validation criteria.
Commitment Letter & SBTi Submission
Preparation of the SBTi commitment letter, target submission forms and all supporting documentation. We manage the SBTi online portal submission and respond to SBTi reviewer queries during the validation process.
Decarbonisation Roadmap
Post-validation roadmap identifying specific emission reduction initiatives, capital investment requirements, timelines and KPIs to track annual progress against your validated SBTi targets — turning a commitment into an actionable internal programme.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Near-term targets cover the next 5–10 years (typically to 2030) and require Scope 1+2 absolute reductions of at least 42% and Scope 3 reductions of at least 25% from a base year, consistent with a 1.5°C pathway. Net-zero targets — validated under the SBTi Corporate Net-Zero Standard — require 90% absolute reduction in Scope 1, 2 and 3 emissions by 2050 at the latest, with residual emissions addressed through permanent carbon removal (not offset credits). Near-term targets are mandatory; net-zero targets are best practice. We recommend setting both simultaneously, as the near-term target modelling is a direct input to the net-zero pathway design.

Scope 3 targets are required only if Scope 3 emissions represent 40% or more of your total Scope 1+2+3 footprint. For many manufacturing companies with significant direct energy use, Scope 1+2 dominate and Scope 3 targets are not required — though SBTi strongly encourages them. You can commit to SBTi before completing your full Scope 3 inventory, as you have 24 months after commitment to submit validated targets. However, we recommend completing the Scope 3 screening before committing, so you are not surprised by a mandatory Scope 3 target requirement. We conduct the full Scope 3 materiality screening as part of our kickoff phase.

The SME fast-track uses a standardised commitment rather than full sector-specific target modelling, allowing SMEs to commit and have their targets validated much faster and at lower cost. The SME commitment requires agreeing to reduce Scope 1 and 2 emissions in half before 2030 and achieving net-zero before 2050 — a fixed commitment rather than a customised pathway. We guide Indian SMEs through the SME eligibility assessment and submission process, which is substantially faster than the full corporate pathway.

This is one of the most important distinctions in corporate sustainability. “Carbon neutral” claims typically offset Scope 1 and 2 emissions using purchased carbon credits — without requiring any actual emission reductions. “Net-zero” without SBTi validation is an unverified claim with no standardised methodology. SBTi specifically requires actual emission reductions — not offset credits — to meet 42% Scope 1+2 reduction targets by 2030 and 90% by 2050. Carbon removal can address only residual emissions (maximum 10%) at net-zero, not replace the reduction requirement. This makes SBTi the only globally recognised standard that prevents “net-zero” from being used as a greenwashing shield without genuine decarbonisation.

SBTi requires annual public progress reporting against your validated targets. If a company fails to make sufficient progress without a legitimate documented reason (such as major M&A restructuring), SBTi can revoke your validated target status and remove you from the SBTi website — a significant reputational consequence. This is why we build the decarbonisation roadmap as part of every engagement: targets that are validated but not backed by concrete, funded reduction initiatives create substantial delivery risk. Our roadmap identifies initiatives with sufficient combined reduction potential to hit near-term targets, with contingency initiatives built in for years where primary initiatives underperform.
