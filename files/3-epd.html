Lifecycle stages

What an EPD covers —
cradle to grave

An EPD quantifies environmental impacts across the full product lifecycle in accordance with ISO 14044 LCA methodology and the applicable Product Category Rules (PCR).

A1–A3
Product Stage
Raw material extraction, transport to factory, and manufacturing process impacts.
A4–A5
Construction / Installation
Transport to site and installation or assembly process environmental loads.
B1–B7
Use Stage
In-use emissions, energy and water use, maintenance, repair and refurbishment over service life.
C1–C4
End of Life
Deconstruction, transport to waste facility, waste processing and disposal impacts.
D
Beyond Boundary
Net benefits from reuse, recovery and recycling potential beyond the system boundary.
Why it matters

EPDs are becoming
a market access requirement.

Green building projects, EU public procurement and global buyers increasingly require EPDs as mandatory documentation — and the EU Green Deal is accelerating this across manufacturing sectors.

01
Green Building Mandates
LEED v4+, BREEAM, GRIHA and Green Star all award credits for products with verified EPDs. Developers specifying products for certified buildings actively require EPDs from manufacturers — and will substitute suppliers who cannot provide them.
02
EU Public Procurement
The EU’s Green Public Procurement (GPP) criteria and Construction Products Regulation increasingly require EPDs for products supplied to EU public sector projects. Indian exporters of steel, cement, aluminium and building materials are directly affected.
03
CBAM & Carbon Footprint Support
An EPD with a verified LCA produces the product-level embedded emissions data that CBAM-regulated exporters need. It also forms the foundation for a Product Carbon Footprint (PCF) certification and satisfies CSRD Scope 3 supplier data requests.
04
Competitive Sustainability Claim
An EPD is a third-party verified, quantitative sustainability claim that goes far beyond marketing language. It enables you to make specific, credible statements about your product’s environmental performance — and prevents greenwashing accusations.
What we do

From LCA to
registered EPD

We manage the complete EPD development process: LCA study, EPD drafting, third-party critical review, Programme Operator registration and publication on the global EPD registry — delivering a verified, internationally recognised EPD for your product.

Product Category Rules (PCR) Identification
We identify the applicable PCR for your product type, select the appropriate Programme Operator (IBU, EPD International, GreenTag, BRE) and define the system boundary, functional unit and scope of the LCA study.
Life Cycle Assessment (LCA)
Full ISO 14044-compliant LCA covering all relevant lifecycle stages. We collect primary data from your production process and supplement with ecoinvent background data, quantifying 15+ environmental impact categories including GWP, AP, EP, ODP and POCP.
EPD Drafting
Preparation of the EPD document in Programme Operator format, covering all mandatory content: product description, system boundary, data quality assessment, LCA results table, and all required EN 15804 + A2 impact category declarations.
Independent Critical Review
Coordination of the mandatory ISO 14044-required critical review by an independent, qualified LCA practitioner or review panel — a prerequisite for EPD registration and publication.
Programme Operator Registration & Publication
Submission to and registration with your chosen accredited Programme Operator, resulting in a published EPD with a unique registration number valid for five years and accessible to buyers globally via the EPD registry.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

A Product Carbon Footprint (PCF) quantifies only the greenhouse gas emissions (GWP) associated with a product across its lifecycle — expressed in kg CO²e per functional unit. An EPD is broader: it reports 15+ environmental impact categories including GWP, acidification, eutrophication, ozone depletion, photochemical oxidant formation and resource depletion, all verified by a third party and published under an accredited Programme Operator. An EPD contains the PCF data as one of its impact categories. For CBAM purposes, the GWP data from an EPD can directly support embedded emissions declarations, but CBAM requires installation-level verified data rather than product-category averages.

EPDs are valid for 5 years from the date of publication by the Programme Operator. After expiry, the EPD is withdrawn from the active registry. To maintain an active EPD, you must update the underlying LCA study and resubmit for a new 5-year registration. The update process is substantially faster than a first EPD — typically 4–8 weeks — because the modelling framework and data collection templates already exist. We track your EPD expiry dates and begin renewal preparation at least 3 months in advance to ensure continuous availability for procurement responses.

The choice of Programme Operator (PO) depends on your target markets, product type and applicable Product Category Rules. IBU (Institut Bauen und Umwelt) is strongest for the German and Central European construction market. EPD International (The International EPD System) has the broadest global recognition. GreenTag is widely recognised in Australia and the Asia-Pacific region. BRE is strong for BREEAM-focused UK and European projects. We recommend the PO based on where your primary buyers are located and which green building schemes they specify — and in many cases we recommend registering with two POs simultaneously to maximise market coverage.

Yes — under most PCRs, a single EPD can declare results for multiple product variants (different grades, thicknesses, compositions) within the same product family, provided the variants share the same functional unit and system boundary. The EPD then declares separate LCA results for each variant. This is significantly more cost-efficient than producing individual EPDs per variant and is the approach we recommend for manufacturers with product ranges. Some PCRs do restrict this — particularly EN 15804 for construction products — and we check applicability during the scoping phase before committing to a product range approach.
