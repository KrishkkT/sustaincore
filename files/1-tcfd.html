Why it matters

Climate is a financial risk.
TCFD makes that legible.

The era of treating climate risk as a non-financial issue is over. TCFD gives investors, lenders and regulators a standardised framework to assess how climate change threatens your business model.

01
Investor Demand
Over $97 trillion in institutional assets are managed by organisations that have publicly committed to TCFD. They require TCFD-aligned disclosures from portfolio companies to assess climate-related financial exposure.
02
Regulatory Trajectory
TCFD is already mandatory in the UK, EU (via CSRD) and Australia. India’s SEBI has embedded TCFD principles in BRSR. Voluntary disclosure today builds mandatory compliance readiness for tomorrow.
03
Lender & Rating Agency Scrutiny
Green bond frameworks, sustainability-linked loans and ESG credit ratings all require TCFD-aligned climate risk assessments as part of their due diligence and ongoing monitoring processes.
What we do

TCFD-aligned climate risk
analysis and disclosure

We deliver the four-pillar TCFD framework — governance, strategy, risk management, metrics — with quantified financial exposure and scenario-tested strategy.

Climate Scenario Analysis
We conduct scenario analysis across 1.5°C, 2°C and 4°C pathways using IEA, IPCC and NGFS scenarios — translating physical and transition risk into quantified financial exposure for your specific assets and markets.
Physical Risk Assessment
Acute risks (floods, cyclones, heatwaves) and chronic risks (sea level rise, temperature shifts, rainfall changes) are mapped to your operating locations using WRI Aqueduct, CLIMADA and peer-reviewed hazard datasets.
Transition Risk Assessment
Regulatory, technology and market transition risks are assessed under each scenario — carbon pricing impacts, stranded asset risk, technology disruption and changing demand patterns for your products.
Governance Structure Review
We review your board’s oversight of climate risk, management responsibilities, incentive structures and reporting lines — designing the governance framework TCFD requires and institutional investors inspect.
Financial Impact Quantification
Risk exposures are translated into financial language — EBITDA impact, capital expenditure requirements, asset impairment scenarios — giving your board and investors a quantified basis for strategy decisions.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

TCFD is not yet formally mandatory for Indian companies as a standalone requirement, but SEBI has embedded TCFD principles within the BRSR framework — making TCFD-aligned disclosures de facto mandatory for top listed companies. Beyond SEBI, lenders offering green bonds or sustainability-linked loans increasingly require TCFD disclosure. Given the pace of global regulatory adoption (mandatory in UK, EU, Australia), Indian companies with global investors or customers should treat TCFD as a near-term mandatory requirement.

Climate scenario analysis tests your business strategy against multiple plausible futures — a 1.5°C world where rapid decarbonisation drives regulatory and market change, a 2°C world with moderate transition, and a 4°C world with severe physical climate impacts. For each scenario, we quantify the financial exposure to your specific assets, supply chain and markets. Without scenario analysis, you cannot meaningfully assess whether your current strategy is resilient to climate change — which is precisely what TCFD, and increasingly regulators, require.

ISSB IFRS S2 (Climate-related Disclosures) is directly built on TCFD’s four-pillar framework. A company that has implemented TCFD is therefore substantially ready for IFRS S2 compliance. The key additional IFRS S2 requirements are more explicit quantitative financial impact disclosure and physical risk disclosure at asset level. Our TCFD reports are designed with IFRS S2 readiness built in, so you are not required to redo significant work when ISSB becomes mandatory in your jurisdiction.
