CEMS — CPCB Mandated

CEMS — Continuous Emission Monitoring Systems — are automated systems that measure and transmit real-time stack emission data 24×7 to the CPCB’s central data server. They aremandatory for 17 categories of large industries under CPCB’s 2014 Direction and subsequent amendments.Non-compliant industries face automatic flagging, penalties and closure notices. We provide end-to-end CEMS services: system selection, installation, calibration, CPCB connectivity, AMC and regulatory compliance management.

System architecture

How a CEMS works —
probe to CPCB server

A complete CEMS comprises four interconnected subsystems that together provide legally compliant, continuous, tamper-evident emission data transmitted directly to the CPCB central server.

→
Sampling Probe & Conditioning
Extractive probe inserted into the stack draws a representative gas sample. Sample conditioning system removes moisture and particulates before analysis.
→
Gas Analysers
Certified gas analysers measure SO₂, NOₓ, CO, CO₂, O₂ and particulates in real time. Must be QAL1-certified under EN 14181 / CPCB specifications.
→
Data Acquisition System (DAS)
DAS collects, timestamps and stores all analyser outputs. Calculates 15-minute averages, daily averages and emission loads. Includes tamper-evident logging.
CPCB Server Connectivity
Secure, encrypted data transmission to the CPCB central data server every 15 minutes. GPRS/internet connectivity with automatic retransmission on failure.
CPCB mandated industries

Is your industry required
to install CEMS?

The CPCB has mandated CEMS for 17 major polluting industry categories under its 2014 Direction. The following industries must have operational CEMS transmitting data to the CPCB server.

Thermal Power Plants
SO₂, NOₓ, PM, CO, O₂, stack gas flow rate, opacity
Cement Plants
PM, SO₂, NOₓ — kiln, raw mill and clinker cooler stacks
Iron & Steel (Integrated)
PM, SO₂, NOₓ — sinter plant, blast furnace, coke oven stacks
Pulp & Paper Industry
PM, SO₂, TRS (Total Reduced Sulphur), NOₓ — recovery boiler
Fertiliser Plants
PM, SO₂, NOₓ, NH₃ — prilling towers, granulators, boilers
Petrochemicals & Refineries
SO₂, NOₓ, CO, PM, H₂S — process heaters and boilers
Chlor-Alkali Industry
Cl₂, HCl, PM — scrubbing stacks and process vents
Common Hazardous Waste Incinerators
PM, SO₂, NOₓ, CO, HCl, dioxins/furans (periodic), opacity
Why it matters

Non-functional CEMS means
automatic non-compliance.

The CPCB monitors data transmission in real time. Gaps in data, sensor failures or CEMS downtime of more than 10% in any month automatically trigger non-compliance flags — regardless of actual emission levels.

01
Automatic Non-Compliance Flagging
The CPCB’s central server monitors data continuity in real time. Any gap exceeding 15 minutes, data transmission failure, or sensor malfunction is automatically flagged. Industries with more than 10% data unavailability in a month are listed as non-compliant — without any inspector visit.
02
Consent to Operate Linkage
Functional CEMS with active data transmission is a mandatory condition in the CTO of all mandated industries. An inoperative or non-transmitting CEMS is treated as a violation of CTO conditions and can result in notice of consent revocation, irrespective of whether actual emissions are within limits.
03
Real-Time Exceedance Detection
When CEMS data exceeds prescribed limits for two or more consecutive 15-minute averaging periods, the CPCB server automatically generates an exceedance alert. This triggers regulatory correspondence and, in severe cases, immediate show-cause action without waiting for periodic manual monitoring reports.
What we do

Complete CEMS lifecycle —
installation to annual AMC

We provide the full spectrum of CEMS services: system design, equipment supply, installation, CPCB connectivity commissioning, calibration, annual maintenance and regulatory data management.

CEMS Design & Equipment Selection
Selection of QAL1-certified analysers and sampling systems appropriate for your stack conditions (temperature, flow, gas matrix). System design for single or multi-stack installations, including shelter, conditioning and DAS specifications.
Installation & Commissioning
Physical installation of sampling probes, conditioning systems, analysers and DAS. Site acceptance testing, zero and span calibration using certified reference gases, and initial 30-day performance demonstration before CPCB connection.
CPCB Server Connectivity
Configuration of secure GPRS/internet data transmission to the CPCB central server in the required XML/JSON format. Connectivity testing, data validation and generation of the CPCB-required installation certificate and connectivity report.
QAL2 Calibration & AST (Annual Tests)
EN 14181 QAL2 calibration using parallel reference method testing to establish system-specific calibration functions. Annual System Test (AST) with parallel reference method comparison, as required for continued CPCB compliance of the installed system.
Annual Maintenance Contract (AMC)
Scheduled preventive maintenance, consumables replacement (filters, probes, reference gas cylinders), corrective maintenance for analyser failures, data gap management and monthly data availability reports — maintaining minimum 90% CPCB data availability.

FAQ

Frequently asked
questions

CEMS is mandated by CPCB for 17 major industry categories under its 2014 Direction and subsequent amendments. The mandated categories include: thermal power plants, cement plants, integrated iron and steel, petroleum refineries, fertiliser plants, pulp and paper mills, sugar industries, distilleries, chlor-alkali plants, caustic soda plants, common effluent treatment plants (CETPs), common biomedical waste treatment facilities, common hazardous waste incineration and treatment facilities, glass manufacturing, tanneries, zinc and lead smelters, and copper smelters. The specific stacks, parameters and transmission requirements vary by sector. You should also check whether your SPCB or individual CTO specifies CEMS requirements beyond the national mandate, as several states have expanded the list.

The CPCB server automatically records data gaps. If transmission is interrupted for more than 15 minutes, the gap is logged. If total data unavailability exceeds 10% in any calendar month, the industry is automatically classified as non-compliant for that month — regardless of actual emission levels. Persistent non-compliance triggers a show-cause notice. Under our AMC, we monitor transmission health 24×7 and dispatch engineers within 4 hours of any system fault to restore connectivity and minimise data gaps. We also maintain substitute measurement data using parallel portable analysers for periods where the CEMS is unavailable for maintenance, per CPCB data substitution guidelines.

These are three quality assurance levels defined under European Standard EN 14181, which the CPCB has adopted as the reference for CEMS quality assurance in India. QAL1 is the type-approval certification of the analyser equipment itself — demonstrated by the manufacturer before sale. Only QAL1-certified analysers are accepted for CPCB-mandated CEMS. QAL2 is the site-specific calibration of a QAL1-certified analyser installed at your particular stack — conducted after installation using parallel reference method measurements. It establishes site-specific calibration functions accounting for your gas matrix. AST (Annual Surveillance Test) is a repeat parallel reference method test conducted annually to confirm that the QAL2 calibration remains valid. All three are required for a CPCB-compliant CEMS installation.
