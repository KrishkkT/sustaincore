Consent toOperate (CTO)

Consent to Operate — also known as Consolidated Consent & Authorisation (CCA) — is the mandatory GPCB permit that every industry must obtain before commencing production or operations . Operating without a valid CTO exposes your business to show-cause notices, closure directions, plant sealing and prosecution under the Water Act 1974 and Air Act 1981. We manage fresh CTO applications, renewals and consent amendments across all categories.

Why it matters

Operating without CTO
is a criminal offence.

A CTO is not a formality — it is the operating licence that permits your factory to run. GPCB has broad enforcement powers against non-compliant units that extend well beyond financial penalties.

Immediate Closure Risk
GPCB can issue a closure direction and seal plant and machinery for units operating without valid CTO or after CTO expiry. Such actions have a direct, irreversible impact on production schedules, customer commitments and contract obligations.
Criminal Prosecution
Operating without consent is punishable under Sections 43 and 44 of the Water Act and Section 21 of the Air Act — with provisions for imprisonment of responsible persons including directors, managers and officers in charge of the facility.
Finance & Insurance Exposure
Lenders, insurers and export buyers frequently audit CTO validity as part of ongoing compliance checks. An expired or missing CTO can trigger loan covenant violations, insurance voidance and procurement blacklisting.

FAQ

Frequently askedquestions

Operating after CTO expiry is an offence under the Water Act 1974 and Air Act 1981 and can attract a show-cause notice, closure direction and prosecution. GPCB also has the authority to seal plant and machinery. Filing before expiry — ideally 30–90 days before — is essential to avoid any gap. We track renewal windows for all clients and initiate the process 90 days before expiry to ensure continuity of consent coverage at all times.

Processing times vary by category and GPCB regional office workload. A well-prepared application for Green or Orange category typically receives consent within 30–60 days of submission. Red category applications involve a site inspection and additional scrutiny, typically taking 60–90 days. Incomplete applications or slow query responses can extend timelines significantly. First-time accuracy in document preparation is the single most important factor in keeping the timeline on track.

Any expansion, addition of new products, installation of additional DG sets or boilers, or significant change in raw material or process requires a consent amendment. Operating an expanded facility without amendment is a violation. We assess whether your proposed change requires a fresh CTE or an amendment to existing consent and manage the application accordingly. When in doubt, it is always safer to file for amendment — the risk of operating without it far exceeds the cost of the application.

CCA — Consolidated Consent & Authorisation — is GPCB's current unified consent format that combines the Consent to Operate under the Water Act, Consent under the Air Act, and Hazardous Waste Authorisation (where applicable) into a single document. The term CTO is commonly used to refer to the operating consent, but in practice most industries in Gujarat today receive a CCA rather than separate consents under each Act. This consolidation simplifies compliance but requires careful management to ensure all components of the CCA remain valid and in sync.

e-Gujcop is GPCB's online portal for all consent and authorisation applications, compliance report submissions and fee payments. All GPCB applications must now be submitted online through e-Gujcop — physical submissions are no longer accepted. We manage the complete portal workflow including account setup, application filling, document upload, fee calculation and real-time status tracking on your behalf, so you never have to navigate the portal directly.
