Strategic Context

What is CBAM and
why does it matter now?

The Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (CBAM), established by Regulation (EU) 2023/956, places a carbon price on imports of certain carbon-intensive goods into the EU. It ensures imported goods face the same carbon cost as EU-produced goods under the EU Emissions Trading System (ETS).

After a 27-month transitional reporting phase, CBAM entered its definitive phase on 1 January 2026. Your EU importer must now purchase and surrender CBAM certificates equal to the embedded emissions in goods imported from you.

The correct CBAM cost formula per Regulation (EU) 2023/956 is based on a phase-out of ETS free allowances: 2.5% in 2026, rising to 100% by 2034.

€100/tCO₂ Penalty

Non-compliance leads to uncapped penalties. The obligation to surrender certificates remains even after penalty payment.

Certificate Sale: 1 Feb 2027

CBAM certificates for 2026 imports open for sale via the central platform. First surrender deadline: 30 Sept 2027.

ISO 14065 Verification

All data must be verified by an EU-accredited third party. Year 1 requires a mandatory on-site inspection.

Implementation Roadmap

CBAM Evolutions

Phase 01

2023 – 2025

Transitional Phase

  • • Quarterly reporting on embedded emissions.
  • • No financial obligation (no certificates).
  • • Default values allowed without limit (regulations apply).
Phase 02

2026 – 2027

Definitive Launch

  • • Mandatory verification by EU accredited verifiers.
  • • Phase-in factor starts at 2.5% reduction.
  • • First annual declaration due 31 May 2027.
Phase 03

2028 – 2033

Scope Expansion

  • • Inclusion of indirect emissions for more sectors.
  • • Gradual phase-out of free ETS allowances.
  • • Possible scope expansion to chemicals/polymers.
Phase 04

2034 Onwards

Full Maturity

  • • 100% CBAM liability (No ETS free allowances).
  • • Full alignment with EU carbon leakage goals.
  • • Mature secondary certificate market.

Regulatory FAQ

The price of CBAM certificates is calculated based on the weekly average auction price of EU ETS allowances. The European Commission publishes this reference price every Monday for the following week.
Article 9 of Regulation (EU) 2023/956 allows importers to claim a reduction in the number of CBAM certificates to be surrendered if a carbon price has already been effectively paid in the country of origin. This requires verified evidence of the payment and that no export rebate was received.
While the definitive phase starts on 1 Jan 2026, the first annual CBAM declaration covering 2026 imports must be submitted by 31 May 2027. Certificates must be surrendered at the time of declaration.
For the start of the definitive phase (Jan 2026), indirect emissions (Scope 2) are included for Cement, Fertilisers, and Electricity. For Iron, Steel, Aluminium and Hydrogen, only direct emissions are currently in scope, but this will likely expand in 2028.
